Daily Non-Negotiables

→3L water every day
→Dinner before 8:30pm strictly
→Weigh yourself every morning after clearing stomach
→Reduce salt — causes water retention and bloating
→8 hours sleep — non negotiable
→Stop at 1900 cal — don't overeat

✅ Always Do

✓Drink 3L water — first glass right after waking up
✓Eat breakfast within 1 hour of waking up
✓Include at least 25g protein in every meal
✓Eat dinner strictly before 8:30pm
✓Weigh yourself every morning after clearing stomach
✓Use only ghee, olive oil or coconut oil for cooking
✓Eat slowly — helps digestion and prevents overeating
✓Sleep 8 hours minimum — growth happens during sleep
✓Add fiber to every meal — vegetables, seeds, dal
✓Black coffee before workout — no sugar
✓Sunday meal prep — cook bulk for the week
✓Eat fruits in the morning — not at night
✓Reduce salt — excess causes bloating and water weight
✓Stop eating at 80% full — not completely stuffed

✕ Never Do

✕Sugar in any form — no sweets, soft drinks, juices, biscuits
✕Processed and packaged foods
✕Fried foods — samosas, chips, vadas, fried chicken
✕Regular cooking oils — sunflower, vegetable, refined oils
✕Eating outside for first 2 months
✕Dinner after 8:30pm — late eating stores fat
✕Skipping meals — leads to overeating later
✕Eating in front of TV or phone
✕Overeating even healthy food — 1900 cal max
✕Alcohol — empty calories, kills recovery
✕Maida, white bread, refined flour products
✕Fruit juices — eat whole fruits instead, juice removes fiber

😴 Why Sleep Is Non-Negotiable

→Muscle is built during sleep, not during the workout
→Growth hormone peaks 10pm-2am — you must be asleep
→Poor sleep raises cortisol — a fat-storing stress hormone
→Less than 6 hours = slower metabolism + higher hunger next day
→Target 8 hrs. 7 acceptable. Less than 6 is counterproductive.

✅ Sleep Dos

✓Same sleep and wake time every day including weekends
✓Keep room dark and cool — ideal 18-20°C
✓Stop caffeine by 2pm — stays in system 6-8 hours
✓Dinner before 8:30pm — digesting food disrupts deep sleep
✓10 min walk after dinner — aids digestion and sleep quality
✓Magnesium glycinate before bed — improves sleep depth
✓Phone away 30 min before sleep — blue light delays melatonin

✕ Sleep Killers

✕Coffee after 2pm
✕Heavy meal close to bedtime
✕Scrolling phone in bed
✕Alcohol — kills REM sleep even if it makes you drowsy
✕Irregular sleep schedule on weekends
✕Bright lights after 9pm — use dim/warm lighting

🔄 Recovery Days

→Light activity only — walk, stretch, household chores
→Eat at maintenance — don't drastically cut on rest days
→Stretch 10 mins — prevents soreness buildup
→Hydrate well — muscles repair faster with good hydration
→Don't feel guilty for resting — rest IS part of the plan

🗓️ What To Expect Week by Week

→You won't look different in Week 1. Completely normal.
→Results order: Feel better → Sleep better → Look better
→Scale may go UP first — water retention. Ignore it.
Week 1 — The Hardest
🔸 Sugar cravings strong on days 2-4
🔸 Low energy, possible headaches from sugar withdrawal
🔸 Muscles sore after first gym session
🔸 Digestion may feel off as body adjusts
What to do: Push through. Drink extra water. Sleep well. This passes by day 5-6.
Week 2 — Getting Easier
🔸 Sugar cravings drop significantly
🔸 Energy starts stabilising throughout the day
🔸 Gym starts feeling like routine, not punishment
🔸 Sleep quality improves noticeably
What to do: Add slightly more weight in gym. Notice small wins.
Week 3 — First Changes
🔸 Face starts slimming — most visible first change
🔸 Clothes feel slightly looser
🔸 Noticeably more energy all day
🔸 Gym strength returning fast
What to do: Take progress photos. Compare to Day 1. Let it fuel you.
Week 4 — It's Becoming Routine
🔸 Habits forming — gym feels automatic
🔸 Eating clean feels normal, not restrictive
🔸 Visible muscle definition starting to return
🔸 Motivation now driven by results, not willpower
What to do: Review plan. Consider adding 4th workout day if feeling strong.

🧠 Mental Rules for Month 1

→One bad meal is not a bad week — never spiral
→Don't compare your Day 7 to someone else's Year 2
→Soreness means you worked — not a reason to skip
→The scale lies short term — trust the process
→Ask yourself every night: did I do better than yesterday?
→You lost 15kg before. You already know you can do this.
